# Sale-performance
Sales performance dashboard built with Excel and validated using SQL, providing insights into revenue, customer behavior and product performance.

### Background Overview

This project presents a comprehensive Sales Performance Dashboard built using Microsoft Excel and validated with SQL queries. The goal of this analysis is to provide clear visibility into business performance across key metrics such as revenue, orders, product performance, and customer behavior.
The dashboard is designed to support data-driven decision-making, helping businesses identify trends, monitor operational efficiency, and uncover growth opportunities.
This project also demonstrates my ability to combine data cleaning, analysis, and visualization skills which are essential for roles in data entry, data analysis, reporting, and business operations.

### Data Structure Overview 

Data Structure Overview
The dataset used for this analysis is structured around transactional sales data and includes the following key fields:
Order ID - Unique identifier for each transaction
Order Date – Date the order was placed
Product Name – Item purchased (e.g., Laptop, Printer, Chair)
Category – Product grouping
Quantity Sold – Number of units sold
Revenue – Total value of each transaction
Payment Method – Cash, Credit Card, Debit Card, Online, Gift Card
Order Status – Delivered, Cancelled, Returned, Pending, Shipped
Referral Source – Channel through which customer was acquired (Instagram, Facebook, Email, Google, Referral)
Data was cleaned and validated using SQL to ensure:
No duplicate records
Accurate aggregation of revenue and quantities
Consistency across all calculated metrics

### Executive Summary 

Executive Summary
The dashboard reveals overall business performance with the following highlights:
Total Revenue: $552,643
Total Orders: 510
Total Quantity Sold: 1,524
Key observations:
Revenue is strong but inconsistent across months, with noticeable peaks and dips
A significant number of orders are cancelled (107) and returned (102), indicating operational inefficiencies
Cash and card payments dominate revenue, while online payments are slightly lower
Certain products like Printers and Chairs generate the highest revenue
Social media channels (Instagram & Facebook) are major contributors to revenue


### Insights

- Monthly Revenue Trends
Revenue fluctuates throughout the year, with May recording the highest performance
April and September show noticeable drops, suggesting possible seasonal or operational issues
Lack of consistent growth trend indicates opportunities for better planning and forecasting
- Order Status Analysis
Pending orders (117) are the highest, which may indicate delays in processing
Cancelled (107) and Returned (102) orders are significantly high, impacting revenue and customer satisfaction
Delivered orders (89) are relatively low compared to total orders
This suggests inefficiencies in order fulfillment and customer experience
- Revenue by Payment Method
Cash ($137K) is the most used payment method
Credit and Gift Cards also contribute significantly
Online payments are the lowest, indicating potential trust or usability issues
- Product Performance
Top-performing products:
Printer (~$95K)
Chair (~$87K)
Tablet & Laptop also perform strongly
Lower-performing products:
Desk and Phone
 Product demand is uneven, suggesting opportunities for inventory optimization
- Referral Source Performance
Instagram and Facebook generate the highest revenue
Email and Google contribute moderately
Referral sources show that digital marketing channels are key drivers of sales


### Recommendations

Based on the analysis, the following actions are recommended:
- Improve Order Fulfillment
Reduce pending, cancelled, and returned orders
Investigate causes (logistics, product quality, communication issues)
Streamline delivery processes
- Optimize Payment Experience
Encourage online payments by improving trust and usability
Offer incentives (discounts, cashback) for digital transactions
- Leverage High-Performing Products
Increase marketing and stock for top-selling items (Printers, Chairs)
Bundle slow-moving products with popular ones
- Strengthen Marketing Channels
Invest more in Instagram and Facebook campaigns
Optimize ad targeting to maximize ROI
- Address Revenue Fluctuations
Investigate low-performing months
Implement seasonal promotions and campaigns
Use forecasting techniques for better planning
